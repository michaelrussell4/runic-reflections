Job Well Done

Capricious fait accomplis unfold
An unjust reckoning to behold
For sovereign judgement he pines
 Incredulous seeking for silver lines
On his heart ambivalence grinds
A meager hope his loyalty finds
But bellicose bouts bite bitterly
Crumbling the foundations of liberty
He petitions his case with audacity
For asylum, an answer for mendacity
Pernicious plights pillage his power
Bogarted, thwarted, his hope they devour
His wretched body, what carceral confines
For sovereign judgement he pines
 A bellowing breeze, will he God appease?
His prayer is lulled, marveling, but at ease

Notes

This poem is about the Biblical character Job. He suffers, he petitions, and the answer that comes doesn't really address the poignent moral qualms Job had argued. Nevertheless, Job seems contented and the Lord rewards him seven-fold for all his losses. At the beginning, "pine" is used in the sense of being worn away or wearied, but in the latter use it infers the meaning of yearning, rather.
