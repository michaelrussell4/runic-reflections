Title: Our Holy Skeuomorph
Date: 2026/06/22 01:27PM
Category: Poetry
Author: Mike Russell
Tags: religious, philosophical, existential, reflective

> - I have for you a riddle
> - I fear to tell you of
> - For some cannot abide it
> - Nor suffer it with love
> - So rather I will answer
> - And leave the question mute
> - Perhaps you can discern it
> - If only you're astute
> - I now proceed to answer
> - A riddle with a puzzle
> - The answer in and of itself
> - May understanding muddle
> - The holy tabernacle
> - The body of our God
> - Is a sacred vestige
> - Though it may seem flawed
> - A skeuomorph it is
> - A consecrated trace
> - A nod to His beginning
> - The start of a human race
> - A digital clock with hands
> - A modern phone with a chime
> - Wood designs on plastic signs
> - The archetypes of time

## Notes

A provocative and radical consideration apparent to the unique individual who believes as I do that God the Father has a body of flesh and bone, after which we are imaged, and also that the human body is rife with vestigial traces persuasively pointing to an origin of evolution, is the question of why God would Himself have such a form, so evocative of a crude path of creation when He might have chosen one more optimal? The proposed answer is that this form carries significant sentiment and importance as a primal artifact of The Origins of gods, man, and life. Additionally, it might be considered that the possession of such does not limit God deleteriously.
