Title: Mournful Mary
Date: 2024/12/09 10:13AM
Category: Poetry
Author: Mike Russell
Tags: melancholic, narrative, reflective, serious

> - Mournful mary
> - Lived in melancholy
> - Others would ask
> - Why she was aghast
> - She'd answer them sorrowfully
> - <br>
> - How some years prior
> - There'd been a fire
> - And in it her doll
> - Thrown in by Paul
> - An evil, wretched squire
> - <br>
> - "So", she explained
> - "My heart is stained"
> - Weeping in agony
> - Telling this tragedy
> - 'til she was old and drained
> - <br>
> - Years passed by
> - Mary cried
> - <br>
> - Then came a man
> - With something in hand
> - It was her doll
> - The man—old Paul
> - "You left it out in the sand"
> - <br>
> - "Oh me, oh my!"
> - Was Mary's reply
> - "I thought she was burned"
> - "My joy you spurned!"
> - "How dare you be so wry"
> - <br>
> - "I threw in a ball"
> - "Not your old doll"
> - Paul remonstrated
> - Mary's tears abated
> - "Days later I found this doll"
> - <br>
> - Mary's eyes grew wide
> - Her heart filled with pride
> - But she let out a sigh
> - All this sadness rife
> - On a mistaken lie
> - <br>
> - The doll was unharmed
> - Her sorrow disarmed
> - But the years she had lost
> - Were a tragic cost
> - A life in sadness consumed
