Gas Light

Poor gal Bella, to be fooled
Lacks suspicion and gets schooled
Old Jack's got her by the throat
He's cunning as the wily goat
A tampering of reality
Paranoia, misery
Jack is really called E.T.
And Bella she is poor old me
Victim of a witty devil
In my insanity he revels
Truth evassive planting doubt
Clarity, assurance I'm ever without

Notes

This poem compares the deeds of Jack to his poor wife, Bella, of Hamilton's play Gas Light (1938) the chicanery of entities deemed extra-terrestrial, and all entities supernatural for that matter, who seem to delight in riding the line between provable existence or non-existance, a form of gaslighting of individuals who feel compelled to believe yet at odds with the matter of proving such existence.
