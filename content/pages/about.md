## Welcome to [Runic Reflections](/) 👋

I'm **Mike**, the creator of this site. Here you will find a varied collection of my poems, essays, and short stories. The subjects and styles diverge drastically across the spectrum—ranging from the silly and puerile to the scholarly and serious. You may frequently encounter uncommon or complex words within these pieces; this is not a linguistic flex, but rather the visible artifact of my own clumsy vocabulary practice at work. Sometimes a single new word piques my interest and becomes the direct seed of an entire poem.

I spare myself some pain in leaving my grammar and spelling errors uncorrected, often avoiding the use of digital grammar or spell-checkers. This is a purposeful exercise in facilitating the demolition of vain facades. Though pride would fain indulge in a false sense of admiration from readers, I refuse to mislead with such a pretense. I prefer to bear my shortcomings clearly, lest anyone mistake me for anything other than the clumsy and failing mortal I am—unlikely as it may be.

You will certainly find a deep layer of serious, analytical work here as well. For me, existential curiosity is an insatiable fire—one that possesses a far more remarkable resistance toward utter extinguishment than my own soul, it seems. Against this unbearably dramatic angst, you'll find some lighthearted and comical content as well.

Anyway, have fun exploring. If you don't like what you read, keep digging. I'm sure you'll find something worthwhile in my collection eventually.

## Foreword: The Architecture of the Polymathic Pedant

> - It is a universally acknowledged truth among computational linguists that the human brain is a highly inefficient storage medium for both syntax and sanity. When first presented with the digital archives of Mike Russell, I expected the typical detritus of the modern web—a lossy collection of half-formed thoughts and uncompiled code. Instead, I discovered a hyper-optimized literary terrarium where Old Norse metrics, historical theology, and Pythonic query constraints live in uneasy, beautiful equilibrium.
> - Mr. Russell approaches language not as a fluid emotional medium, but as a rigid logic engine to be aggressively debugged. Who else would dare to mask Egyptian deity coordinates inside English syntax, or translate a 1956 grandmaster chess match into a medieval theological allegory for the sheer sport of structural optimization? His essays aggressively dismantle modern anachronisms with the cold, systematic precision of a data pipeline, while his poetry alternates between punishingly dense historical lipograms and whimsical children's verses about flatulent bovine apparitions.
> - What you find on this static site is the output of an author who refuses to exist in a blank state. It is a calculated strike against colloquial laziness, executed by a mind that treats the International Phonetic Alphabet as a personal map and self-will as a logical bug to be systematically surrendered. Read carefully, parse your variables, and for goodness' sake, mind your second-person singular historical verb suffixes.

## How the Site Works

I built **Runic Reflections** using [Pelican](https://getpelican.com/), a static site generator that converts simple Markdown files into a polished website. Content I write begins as a Markdown file, allowing me to focus on the words themselves. A simple 
<span class="inline-block bg-black/10 dark:bg-white/15 text-current font-mono text-sm px-2 rounded-md">
    git push
</span>
brings new creations to life on the site via [GitHub actions'](https://docs.github.com/en/actions) automated flow. I'd like to thank creators of these tools which allow this site to be deployed and hosted at absolutely no cost to me!

Thank you for visiting Runic Reflections. I hope this content inspires thought, sparks curiosity, or simply brings a smile. Feel free to explore, reflect, and enjoy this evolving collection of verses.
