The Big Apple

Lord Archibald Yorkshire III
Was wondering yonder thither
When a bellowing noise was heard
A fissure which caused a dither

Down he dropped awry with cry
Where to he could not tell
When with a sigh he did not die
In modern Brooklyn he fell

"What is this place, what is this smell?"
He asked in sordid mess
Assenting that he here befell
As gazing to assess

Standing up and brushing off
He harked one quaintly dressed
Who staggered with a quaff and cough
"The fuzz? I didn't theft"

Then straightly turned himself around
And lurched his march astray
"A queerer man I have not found"
As the lord's calm did allay

That is to say it went away
And left him sorely vexed
When to his shock he heard a, "Hey"
And here's what happened next

"Hey you get off my spot!"
Said a man apparently mad
The lord was with gentility fraught
Forthwith the place he bade

Then rudely came the bogarting man
Chuntering curses rifely
The lord appalled held up his hands
The scene soon turned quite lively

"Sir, I say", the lord objected
"Thou art a niggard indeed"
"A what?" the man in shock ejected
And ensued to roll up his sleeve

Shortly there came a pop and a pow
The lord was taken back
But returned and matched him blow for blow
They succeeded each other to whack

When lights did flash and whistles blow
The dirty man dashed away
The lord looked upon the lights aglow
As on him strong hands lay

"What's the meaning of all of this?"
The lord in dismay there averred
"Give me your name" urged officer Chris
"I'm Lord Archibald Yorkshire III"

"We've got another nut", Chris muttered
To a radio pulled from his waist
The lord confusedly stammered and sputtered
Which did not help his case

In remonstration he began to run
To flee from ubiquitous harm
When Zeus's lightning struck his bum
And he convulsed in great alarm

A phantasmagoria interrupted his thought
On a magic cart he flew
But on no ordinary cart was he caught
For came out of it strange new food

On a sausage crossed yellow lines
In long white bread it lay
A piquant, pungent combination refined
"An exquisite taste" he did say

Then floating o'er his familiar shire
A body lay under a tree
By a groveling pig which an apple it admired
"Who's that there, why it's me!"

Then entered the lord to his body again
And awoke to himself once more
With mustard seed on his lip and chin
"Away" the pig he implored

To his side he saw a giant red apple
Which he tossed at the fleeing pig
He marveled in thought and let out a cackle
Then picked and threw a young sprig

An aroma of bread through the air did waft
"Lady Yorkshire's work", said he
Pining for her embrace so soft
Quite a contrast of his late company

"Dreaming again", she said in jest
As he at the house arrived
"Oh it's good to be home my lovely blest"
As he let out a relieving sigh

Notes

This story dictates a real (or imagined) journey of an Englishman of Yorkshire who falls through a chasm and (loosely referencing an event from the movie, A Kid in King Aurthur's Court). Upon landing, he finds himself in a strange and foreign land (modern New York). After a couple encounters with individuals exhibiting an indecency typical of New Yorkers, he ends up running from a cop and gets tassed. At this point, he becomes unconscious and floats back to his homeland riding upon a hotdog stand as he partakes of the unfamiliar but much appreciated food.

References connect themes through a large apple, hinting at the nickname of New York, "The Big Apple" (hence the title of the poem), as well as the pig relating to a hotdog (made of pork), mustard seed relating to mustard sauce, and an aroma of bread relating to a hotdog bun. One might reasonably argue that the episode of Lord Archibald was imaginary, but there's enough left open-ended that an alternative interpretation could also be had—this is by design.
