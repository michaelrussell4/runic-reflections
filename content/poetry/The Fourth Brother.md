Title: The Fourth Son
Date: 2026/02/20 04:19PM
Category: Short Stories
Tags: adventurous

An old widower had four sons. To the first, he gave his fortune; to the second, he gave his flocks; to the third, he gave his house; but for the fourth son, he had nothing left but an old hen. The youngest took the hen in a sack and went off into the world to make something of himself.

He wandered into a nearby kingdom and heard how the king's daughter had been carried off by a dragon who lived in a cave of clouds, and how the king offered a reward that whosoever could rescue the princess would have the princess, half his kingdom, and become king when the king died.

The widower's son thought he might try his luck and set out into the woods with his hen in his sack to find the tallest tree. He found a very tall tree indeed and climbed to the top; and though he still did not reach the clouds, he was able to see a tall mountain.

As the mountain was taller than the tree, he thought he might try to climb it and be nearer to the princess.

He climbed down the tree and set off toward that mountain. As he came near and climbed the mountain, he discovered a small fire burning which called out:

> Little bird, I'll burn thy feet<br>
> Unless you give me wood to eat.<br>

The man then saw a little bird carrying twigs back and forth from the forest to the fire. The twigs were so small that the fire quickly burned them and would call out for more. The bird wearily obeyed, fearing the threats from the fire. The man said, "I shall help you, little bird." And he felled a tree and set it on the fire, which gratefully began to devour it. The man said, "You are free to go now, little bird." The bird thanked the man and said, "Because of your kindness, I will tell you a secret:

> Beneath the fire you'll find ground;<br>
> Beneath the ground is water clear;<br>
> Beneath the water is darkness found;<br>
> Beneath the darkness an egg is there.<br>

After telling the man this, the bird flew quickly away. Before long, the fire had burned through the whole tree and began crying for more:

> Foolish man, I'll burn thy feet<br>
> Unless you give me wood to eat.<br>

But the man saw how the fire grew small and laughed, saying, "You shall not burn me but shall die." And after the wood ran out, the fire had nothing left to burn and vanished away.

He recalled what the bird had said and decided he must find what was beneath the fire. Using sticks and stones, he dug beneath the ashes where the fire had been until he found water filling the bottom of the hole. The water cried out:

> You've found me but I don't wish to stay;<br>
> Give me a path and I'll run away.<br>

The man took a stick and carved a trench out of the hole, and the water slid down the trench until it had all run out. Now where water had been in the hole, there was darkness. The darkness said:

> Here alone I fear I'll be;<br>
> I want a friend who's just like me.<br>

The man said, "You are not quite alone. There's another just like you. It is called 'night'." The darkness asked where 'night' could be found. The man said, "It is running away from the sun far out west." At that, the darkness crawled out of the hole and scurried out west in search of night.

Once darkness was out of the hole, the man looked within it to find an egg deep down at the bottom. The man pulled the hen out of his sack and dropped it down into the hole. The hen sat upon the egg for a few days, and the man rested and ate berries while he waited. Then the egg hatched and out of it came an eagle, which devoured the old hen, which made it grow big and strong. The eagle came out of the hole and threatened to eat the man, too. But the man said, "I am the fourth son of a widower and I have four questions which I will put forth to you. If you can answer any of them, you may eat me. But if you can answer none, then you shall let me live." The strong eagle agreed.

The man said, "Who lives long while eating little, but dies after eating a feast?" The eagle thought and thought but could not determine the answer and gave up. The man said, "The fire lived long when fed twigs slowly, but after consuming a whole tree, it dwindled and died."

Then the man said, "How did the man move water from a hole without ever touching the water?" The eagle thought and thought but could not determine the answer and gave up. The man said, "I dug a trench with a stick that led away from the hole and the water spilled out until it was gone."

Then the man said, "How was darkness taken out of a hole without sun or flame or light?" The eagle thought and thought but could not determine the answer and gave up. The man said, "The darkness longed for a friend and I sent it away to chase night out west."

Then for the fourth question, the man said, "What came first, the eagle or the egg?" The eagle thought and thought and said, "Every egg comes from its mother, but every mother comes from an egg. Which is first, which is first? I cannot tell." The man said, "The egg I found inside the hole and set my hen upon it. From that egg you hatched, O eagle."

At this, the eagle was astonished. It remarked, "Thou art the wisest man in the land. I shall do one thing you ask of me." The man asked the eagle if it could help him find and slay the dragon that had stolen away the princess. The eagle said it must have a pig, a goat, a sheep, and a cow, and then it could help the man. The man said he had not the money to buy such things. But the eagle said it could find them itself, and it flew away.

The man waited a day and a night and the eagle returned, having grown to a monstrous size. It then agreed to let the man ride upon its back, and they flew high in the sky in search of the dragon's lair in the clouds. They found the lair, and the eagle landed upon the clouds near the entrance.

The eagle screeched and the dragon came out. The man, who had slunk off the back of the eagle, crept into the cave in search of the princess. As he did, the dragon began to advance towards the eagle in a rage. It blew fire at the eagle, but the eagle was not hurt. "I was born of fire and it cannot harm me," said the eagle.

Then the eagle flew at the dragon and scratched out its eyes with its talons. Next, it bit at the dragon's neck until the head was severed from the body. The eagle flew back to the cave where the man was waiting with the princess, whom he carried on the eagle's back. Then the eagle flew down and they met the princess's father, who received them with much gladness. The next day, a large wedding and feast were held in celebration. The man won the princess, half the kingdom, and became king after the princess's father passed.
