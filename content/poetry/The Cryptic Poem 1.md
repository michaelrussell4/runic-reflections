Title: The Cryptic Poem 1
Date: 2019/07/15 10:43AM
Category: Poetry
Author: Mike Russell
Tags: narrative, surreal, whimsical, abstract

> - There was a night I had a dream
> - A puzzle indeed would have a key
> - Which solved a wonderful mystery
> - Now I’ll introduce this scheme
> - <br>
> - My dream began without a start
> - A ceremony of matrimony, a work of art
> - Pascal the man presented his own
> - A soldier who hailed from a flatland home
> - <br>
> - And who was the bride to join this groom
> - To brighten his day and unite ‘till the tomb?
> - She he preferred: Princess Modulo the Third
> - And the audience perceived without a word
> - <br>
> - Combined together I now could see
> - Their union familiar, what could it be?
> - A fame from a game, now what was the name?
> - For knowing it now is crucial, I claim
> - <br>
> - What happened next in this dream of mine?
> - You must know more or you won’t see the sign
> - Brahms stood up and threw a petunia
> - He opened his mouth, “Canción de cuna”
> - <br>
> - Here we are from my dream’s beginning
> - There it ended with Brahms still grinning
> - A clue to the key from the dream sublime
> - A song it makes from sixty four in time
> - <br>
> - To see this key a pen you will need
> - Set down it’s tip on paper, I plead
> - And follow the pattern from the song above
> - Here you will discern the letter I love
> - <br>
> - Now ‘A’ plus itself is ‘B’, add to that two it’s ‘D’
> - That’s a hint for the answer you’ll see
> - Twice our key plus three is first
> - Thrice our key minus six is more
> - <br>
> - Next our key plus two plus four
> - Last, square key and minus a score
> - Now the puzzling question that I pose 
> - is who is the being from which this rose?
