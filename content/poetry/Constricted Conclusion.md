Title: Constricted Conclusion 🐍
Date: 2026/05/19 09:14AM
Category: Poetry
Author: Mike Russell
Tags: existential, philosophical, abstract, reflective

> - The painful disagreement of logic and man's mind
> - Displays itself quite clearly, herein you'll soon find
> - Consider now the question of how it came to be:
> - The universe, the stars, the earth, the land, and sea
> - A clean and simple start: from zero all arose
> - But logic cannot have this; this cannot be, it knows
> - Causes bring a thing, and nothing's not a thing
> - If nought is the beginning, then from it none could spring
> - Rather, logic favors, causes without pause
> - All the way back, forever for all there was
> - How hard this is for man: incomprehensibility
> - He cannot fathom such a thing as endless eternity
> - Let the tail pass through the head—ouroborusly
> - A start, an end, a death, a birth, I see we now agree

## Notes

There is a painful disagreement between logic and human reasoning. Perhaps the latter is a subjective consideration of such—judge this of yourself. Logic states that causes cannot come from nothing. It is disagreeable to suggest that something came from nothing. Hence, logic's preference for the beginning of our universe is an infinite regression that forever avoids the something-from-nothing conundrum. Yet, a human reasons this incomprehensible—no beginning. He prefers a beginning and an end, a pattern with which he's quite familiar. And thus, he chooses comprehension over logic and permits ill-logic to avoid incomprehensibility.
